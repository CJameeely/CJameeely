Inspiration

Background

In the age of algorithmic visibility, the self is no longer a coherent entity but a constantly reassembled construct of profiles, gestures, and data traces. Every scroll, every filtered image, becomes a fragment of identity—duplicated, compressed, and distributed across platforms.

Social media has turned self-perception into performance. The front camera stages an idealized version of the self, while the rear camera reflects a detached observer. Switching between them mirrors a deeper psychological oscillation—between being seen and seeing oneself, between exposure and disappearance.

Artistic Research

The project draws from psychoanalysis and media theory, particularly:

Lacan’s Mirror Stage — self-recognition through reflection that always carries alienation.

Zajonc’s Mere-Exposure Effect — repeated exposure to one’s image breeds both comfort and numbness.

The Gaze Effect — social media turns looking into a looped performance; every viewer participates in constructing identity.

Artistic inspirations include:

Sophie Calle, for her narrative exposure and intimacy.

Rafael Lozano-Hemmer, for interactive installations that visualize surveillance and presence.

Hito Steyerl, for her critique of digital visibility and fractured authenticity.
