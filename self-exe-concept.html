Concept

Self.exe visualizes the fragmentation of identity within digital spectatorship. Through gesture-controlled interactions built in TouchDesigner, the installation captures facial features in real time and decomposes them into floating fragments responsive to hand motion.

As participants interact, their faces multiply and distort across the screen—a reflection not of who they are, but of how the system reads them.

The interface becomes a mirror that returns disassembly instead of wholeness.

Each gesture operates like a command, each movement an algorithmic echo. What appears as control is in fact a choreography of loss—an endless execution of self.
